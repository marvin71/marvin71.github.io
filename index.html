About Me

I am a computer science student at Saarland University. Currently, I am working on my Master's thesis as a member of the Operating Systems Group at the Max Planck Institute for Software Systems under the supervision of Antoine Kaufmann.

As part of my Master's thesis, I am working on SimBricks, a full-system simulation framework. More specifically, I am creating a framework to conduct scalable end-to-end simulations with focus on networking protocols like TCP and its various congestion control algorithms.

Generally, I am interested in Operating Systems, Databases, Programming Languages, Networking, and Distributed Systems. I find it fascinating to learn how computer systems work in detail, especially how software and hardware interact to create a usable, performant system.
